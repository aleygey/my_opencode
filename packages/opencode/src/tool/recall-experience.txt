Search the project's accumulated experience library for relevant guidance,
rules, conventions, or pitfalls captured from prior interactions in this
workspace.

Use proactively when:
- entering an unfamiliar subsystem (first time touching a module)
- before destructive or hard-to-undo operations (deploy, push, schema migration, rm -rf, force push)
- a tool call returned a surprising or non-obvious error
- the user's request mentions a domain you have not worked in this session
- you are about to follow a workflow that may have a project-specific convention

Don't use for:
- trivial questions or small edits inside a well-understood loop
- when the system prompt's <baseline_experiences> or <retrieved_experiences>
  already cover the relevant rule
- general programming knowledge (use codesearch / websearch instead)

The tool runs an LLM-backed search over the workspace's experience graph.
Latency is typically a few seconds. Returns up to `max` matched experiences
plus their auto-expanded preconditions (rules they depend on). Each entry
has a `relevance_reason` so you can scan quickly and decide whether to
follow the rule.

Each returned experience has fields:
- kind: workflow_rule | constraint_or_policy | preference_style | know_how |
        domain_knowledge | pitfall_or_caveat | workflow_gap | custom:*
- title: 10-30 char summary
- statement: machine-readable rule (when present, treat as authoritative)
- abstract: 1-3 sentence detail
- relevance: why this came back (seed match, or precondition of another match)

If the result is empty, the workspace has no captured experience for that
query — proceed with general best practices.
